Know Your Rights

Your HOA Rights, Explained in Plain English

Understanding these five foundational concepts can change everything about how you deal with your HOA.

Most HOA members don't know they have rights. The board counts on that. These guides explain your core legal protections in plain English — no legal degree required. Whether you're facing a fine, fighting a rule change, or trying to attend a meeting you've been excluded from, knowing the rules of the game is the first step to winning it.

Important: These guides are educational. HOA laws vary significantly by state. Always consult a licensed attorney for your specific situation.
☀

The Sunshine Clause

Most states require HOA board meetings to be open to all members. Learn what open meeting laws protect, how to invoke your right to attend, what constitutes a valid executive session — and what to do when the board holds secret meetings.

The Good Faith Clause

Every HOA board is bound by a duty of good faith and fair dealing. Understand the Business Judgment Rule, what bad faith actually looks like — selective enforcement, self-dealing, personal vendettas — and how to build a case when a board crosses the line.

CC&Rs and Bylaws

Your association's governing documents are legally binding contracts — and you have a right to copies of all of them. Understand the hierarchy of HOA authority, how amendments must be passed, and the most common ways boards violate their own rules.

Robert's Rules for HOA Members

Robert's Rules of Order gives members real procedural power at HOA meetings. Learn the six most powerful motions you can make, how to challenge an improperly conducted vote, how to get items on the agenda, and what quorum means for you.

When disaster strikes, understanding who covers what can mean the difference between recovery and ruin. Learn the difference between the HOA master policy and your individual coverage, the walls-in vs. bare-walls distinction, and what to do when the board goes silent.
