State Guides Explained

What to Expect on Each State Page

Each full state guide goes beyond a simple statute list — it explains what the law actually means for you as a member.

📋

Key Statutes

We identify the primary state laws that govern HOAs and condo associations in your state — the planned communities act, condominium statutes, and relevant corporate law — with plain-English explanations of what each covers.

🏛️

Oversight & Enforcement

Many states have a regulatory agency — or a specific division — that oversees HOA and condo associations. We explain who that agency is, what authority it has, and when they can actually help you.

📬

How to File a Complaint

Step-by-step guidance on where to send a complaint, what information you'll need, and what to realistically expect from the process — whether that's a state agency, the courts, or an alternative dispute resolution body.

⚖️

Your Member Rights

Each state guide covers the core rights that matter most: accessing records, attending board meetings, voting on rule changes, challenging fines, and the due process you're entitled to before enforcement action is taken.

🌐

State-Specific Issues

From wildfire insurance in California and Hawaii, to hurricane reserve funds in Florida, to short-term rental conflicts in tourist markets — every state has unique HOA flashpoints we address directly.

🔧

Practical Action Steps

Information alone isn't enough. Each guide ends with concrete next steps: how to find your governing documents, how to send a records request, and where to get help if the board refuses to comply.
