HOA Demand Letter Template

A formal demand letter creates a written record of your request, establishes a deadline, and signals that you know your rights. Copy and customize this template for your situation.

Before You Send: Have this letter reviewed by a licensed attorney in your state before mailing. The legal citations in brackets should be updated to match your specific state statutes. A one-hour attorney consultation typically costs $150–$300 and can significantly strengthen your letter.

[Your Full Name] [Your Street Address] [City, State, ZIP] [Your Phone Number] [Your Email Address] [Date] Via Certified Mail, Return Receipt Requested Tracking #: [Certified Mail Tracking Number] [Board President's Full Name], President Board of Directors [Association Full Legal Name] [Association Mailing Address] [City, State, ZIP] Re: Formal Demand — [Brief One-Line Description of Issue] Dear President [Last Name] and Members of the Board of Directors: I am writing on behalf of myself as a member of [Association Name] to formally demand that the Board of Directors take the following action(s) within [14 / 30] days of the date of this letter: 1. [State your first demand clearly and specifically, e.g., "Provide complete copies of all financial records for fiscal year 2024, including all invoices, contracts, and bank statements."] 2. [State your second demand, if applicable] 3. [State any additional demands] STATEMENT OF FACTS The basis for this demand is as follows: [Set out the relevant facts in clear, chronological order. Use specific dates, amounts, names of board members, and descriptions of what occurred. Attach supporting documentation where available. Keep this section factual — avoid emotional language. Example: "On [Date], I submitted a written records request to the Board via certified mail (tracking # XXXX). As of the date of this letter, [X] business days have elapsed and no response has been received."] LEGAL BASIS FOR THIS DEMAND This demand is made pursuant to: • [Cite the specific section of your state's HOA statute, e.g., "Hawaii Revised Statutes §421J-15 (Member right to inspect records)" or "California Civil Code §5200 (Inspection and copying of records)"] • [Cite the specific section of the Association's CC&Rs or Bylaws, if applicable, e.g., "Article VI, Section 3 of the Association Bylaws"] RESPONSE REQUIRED Please respond in writing to the address listed above within [14 / 30] days from the date of this letter. If the Board fails to respond or comply within the stated period, I will have no choice but to [choose the applicable next steps: "file a formal complaint with the [State HOA Regulatory Body]" / "request formal mediation pursuant to [state statute]" / "retain legal counsel and pursue all available legal and equitable remedies, including attorney's fees where permitted by statute"]. I am prepared to resolve this matter cooperatively and in good faith. However, the Board's failure to act has left me no alternative but to submit this formal demand. Please direct all written responses to my address above. [Optional: "I have retained legal counsel, and you may also direct a copy of your response to: [Attorney Name, Address]."] Respectfully submitted, [Your Signature] [Your Printed Full Name] Member, [Association Name] Lot/Unit #: [Your Property Identifier] Enclosures: [List attached supporting documents, e.g., "Copy of records request dated [Date]", "Copy of certified mail receipt #XXXX"] Sent Via: Certified Mail #[Tracking Number] — Return Receipt Requested

Tips for Sending an Effective Demand Letter

1. Always Send by Certified Mail

USPS Certified Mail with Return Receipt creates a legal record of delivery that the board cannot dispute. Keep the green return receipt card when it comes back — it is evidence that the board received your letter on a specific date.

2. Be Specific, Not Emotional

Boards and their attorneys look for emotional language as a reason to dismiss your letter. State facts: dates, amounts, names, specific actions or failures. The more specific your letter, the harder it is to ignore.

3. Set a Realistic Deadline

14 days is appropriate for urgent matters (records requests already overdue, imminent violation). 30 days is appropriate for most disputes. Courts look more favorably on demands that give the other party reasonable time to respond.

4. Keep a Complete Copy

Keep a copy of every letter you send, every letter you receive, and every certified mail tracking number. Organize these chronologically. This file is your evidence if the dispute escalates.

5. Don't Threaten What You Can't or Won't Do

Only include next steps you are genuinely prepared to take. If you say you'll file with the state regulatory body, be prepared to follow through. Empty threats undermine your credibility.

6. Carbon Copy Strategically

Copying your state's HOA regulatory body or your attorney adds weight. It signals that this letter is not just a venting exercise — it is the beginning of a formal process. Only CC parties who you have actually notified or will notify.

7. Get Attorney Review First

The $150–300 cost of a one-hour attorney review of your demand letter is almost always worth it. An attorney can add the correct legal citations for your state, catch procedural errors, and advise whether your demands are legally supportable.
